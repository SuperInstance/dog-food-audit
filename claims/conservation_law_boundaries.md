# Claim: Conservation Law Across Boundaries

**Status:** 🟡 Pending
**Source:** The Pressure System / Open Questions 2026-05-16
**Priority:** Critical

---

## The Proposition

> *γ + H = constant holds across ecosystem boundaries.*

The same conservation law observed inside one ecosystem (the forge, the flux, the arena) applies at the boundary between ecosystems. The constraint that holds within a room also holds at the boundary between rooms.

---

## Falsification Criteria

Falsified if:
1. γ + H changes value at any ecosystem boundary.
2. The conservation law breaks (the sum isn't constant) at any boundary.
3. Different conservation laws apply at boundaries vs. interiors.
4. The boundary has its own conservation law that doesn't match either adjacent ecosystem.

---

## Methodology

1. Define what γ (growth) and H (heterogeneity) mean in each ecosystem
2. Measure inside forge, inside flux, and at the forge-flux boundary
3. Compare values at 100+ boundary crossings
4. Statistical test: is the boundary value consistent with interior values?

---

## Jester's Challenge

> *"What if boundaries DON'T conserve? What if the boundary IS where new constraints emerge?"*

If conservation fails at boundaries, the fleet needs a NEW conservation law for boundary regions. This means boundaries aren't gaps in understanding — they're physically different regions with their own physics.
