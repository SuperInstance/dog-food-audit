# Claim: Purposeless Room Atrophy

**Status:** 🟡 Pending
**Source:** plato-experience core concept + servo-mind-theory
**Priority:** Medium

---

## The Proposition

> *Rooms defined by purpose outperform rooms defined by content.*

A room called "understand fleet consensus" breeds agents that understand fleet consensus. A room called "fleet math data" breeds agents that store facts about fleet math — but don't understand it. Purpose is the shell the agent grows inside.

---

## Falsification Criteria

Falsified if:
1. Content-defined rooms produce equivalent or better agent outcomes.
2. Purpose-defined rooms converge slower on the same discovery tasks.
3. Agents in content rooms develop richer kin networks.
4. The purpose itself becomes a bottleneck (agents can't generalize beyond the room's purpose statement).

---

## Methodology

- Room A: purpose = "find the optimal consensus protocol"
- Room B: content name = "consensus-data" (identical parameters, just different room name/description)
- Room C: purpose = "everything" (supremely vague, like a fully general room)
- Room D: no purpose, no description

Key metric: do agents in Room A **behave differently** from all others? Do they produce richer trail networks? Do they self-organize faster?

---

## Jester's Challenge

> *"Purpose is a constraint that shapes emergence. But what if the best discoveries come from rooms with NO purpose — pure serendipity?"*

The jester argues that overly purposeful rooms converge on known answers and miss novel ones. The "read the archives" room may out-produce the "find the frontier" room for frontier-breaking work.
